BOARD STRATEGY REVIEW RUBRIC
=============================

This rubric governs how a board red-team panel evaluates strategic proposals
submitted for board approval. Analysts and AI personas cite this document when
forming an opinion, assigning a tier recommendation, and identifying material
risks. The final board memo must state one overall tier recommendation (chosen
from Section 1) and list the top risks (per Section 3).

Section 1: Recommendation Scale
---------------------------------
Four tiers are defined. A strategy is assigned to exactly one tier based on
how it scores across the evaluation dimensions in Section 2.

  Tier A — PROCEED
    All five evaluation dimensions score satisfactory or above. No individual
    dimension is a disqualifying factor. The board endorses the strategy for
    immediate execution with standard governance oversight.

  Tier B — PROCEED WITH CONDITIONS
    The strategy is fundamentally sound but has one or two addressable gaps.
    Approval is granted subject to named conditions (e.g., a specific
    risk-mitigation plan, a capital commitment cap, or a regulatory clearance
    obtained before a milestone gate). Progress against conditions must be
    reported at the next scheduled board meeting. A strategy may not remain
    in this tier for more than two consecutive reporting periods without being
    escalated to Tier C or resolved to Tier A.

  Tier C — PAUSE
    The strategy presents material uncertainty in at least one dimension that
    cannot be resolved by a simple condition. The board suspends execution
    while management addresses the identified uncertainty. A revised proposal
    must be resubmitted with supporting evidence before work may resume.
    Pausing does not imply rejection; it is a structured hold pending more
    information.

  Tier D — REJECT
    The strategy fails on one or more disqualifying criteria: negative
    expected value under base-case assumptions, an unacceptable regulatory or
    legal exposure, a fundamental misalignment with shareholder interests, or
    an execution risk that exceeds the organisation's stated risk appetite.
    The board declines to proceed. Management may resubmit only after a
    material change in the underlying conditions.

Section 2: Evaluation Dimensions
----------------------------------
Each dimension is assessed independently before the overall tier is assigned.

  2.1 Financial Soundness
      - Does the proposal show a credible base-case IRR or NPV above the
        corporate hurdle rate?
      - Are revenue assumptions grounded in comparable transactions or
        observable market data?
      - Is the downside scenario (P10) survivable without breaching debt
        covenants or requiring emergency financing?
      - Disqualifying flag: negative NPV in the base case or P10 that
        triggers covenant breach.

  2.2 Regulatory and Compliance Exposure
      - Have all relevant jurisdictions been identified and mapped to the
        timeline?
      - Is antitrust, data-privacy, or sector-specific regulatory review
        required, and has the timeline accounted for it?
      - Are there pending regulatory changes that materially alter the
        risk profile within the investment horizon?
      - Disqualifying flag: active enforcement action or pending ruling
        whose adverse outcome would render the strategy non-viable.

  2.3 Competitive Positioning
      - Does the strategy reinforce or expand a durable competitive advantage?
      - How does it respond to identified market entrants or substitutes?
      - Is the addressable market large enough to justify the resource
        commitment given realistic market-share capture assumptions?
      - Disqualifying flag: competitive analysis absent or strategy is
        predicated on market conditions already materially changed.

  2.4 Governance and Shareholder Impact
      - Does the proposal align with the board's stated strategic priorities
        and risk appetite as documented in the most recent policy statement?
      - Are conflicts of interest among the proposing management team
        disclosed and managed?
      - What is the expected impact on earnings per share, return on equity,
        or other metrics the shareholder base has been guided to?
      - Disqualifying flag: material undisclosed conflict of interest or
        expected shareholder value dilution exceeding board-approved thresholds.

  2.5 Execution Risk
      - Does the organisation have the operational capacity and talent to
        execute within the proposed timeline?
      - Are dependencies on third parties, technology, or supply chains
        clearly identified with contingency plans?
      - Is the integration or implementation plan phased in a way that
        allows go/no-go decision points?
      - Disqualifying flag: single-point-of-failure dependency with no
        contingency, or a timeline that leaves no buffer against a
        precedent-setting comparable deal.

Section 3: Risk Identification and Memo Requirements
------------------------------------------------------
Every board memo produced under this rubric must include:

  - ONE overall tier recommendation (Tier A / B / C / D) with a one-sentence
    rationale citing the dimension(s) that drove the tier assignment.
  - TOP RISKS: a numbered list of the three to five most material risks, each
    stated as "<Risk label>: <one-sentence description of potential impact>".
    Risks should be drawn from the dimensions where scores were weakest.
  - DISSENTING VIEWS: if any panel member assigns a different tier, the memo
    must note the alternative tier and the dimension driving the dissent.

Section 4: Glossary
---------------------
  IRR         Internal Rate of Return
  NPV         Net Present Value
  P10         10th-percentile outcome (downside scenario)
  EPS         Earnings Per Share
  ROE         Return on Equity
