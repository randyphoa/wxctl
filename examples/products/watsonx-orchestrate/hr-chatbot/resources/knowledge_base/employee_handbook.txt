ACME CORPORATION EMPLOYEE HANDBOOK
===================================

Section 1: Paid Time Off (PTO)
------------------------------
All full-time employees accrue 20 days of paid time off (PTO) per calendar
year, accrued at a rate of 1.67 days per month. After five years of service,
the annual PTO allowance increases to 25 days. Up to 5 unused PTO days may be
carried over into the next calendar year; carried-over days expire on March 31.
PTO requests must be submitted in the HR portal at least two weeks in advance
for absences of three or more consecutive days.

Section 2: Remote Work Policy
-----------------------------
ACME operates a hybrid work model. Employees may work remotely up to three
days per week, with Tuesdays and Thursdays designated as in-office anchor
days. Fully remote arrangements require written approval from both the
employee's manager and HR, and are reviewed annually. Remote employees must
be reachable during core hours (10:00-16:00 local time) and maintain a secure
home workspace that complies with the IT Security Standard (Section 7).

Section 3: Parental Leave
-------------------------
Birth parents are entitled to 16 weeks of fully paid parental leave.
Non-birth parents and adoptive parents are entitled to 8 weeks of fully paid
leave. Parental leave may be taken continuously or split into two blocks
within the first 12 months. To request parental leave, submit the Parental
Leave form in the HR portal at least 30 days before the expected start date.

Section 4: Sick Leave
---------------------
Employees receive 10 paid sick days per year, separate from PTO. Absences of
more than three consecutive sick days require a doctor's note. Unused sick
days do not carry over and are not paid out.

Section 5: Benefits Overview
----------------------------
ACME provides medical, dental, and vision insurance effective on the first
day of employment. The company matches 401(k) contributions up to 5% of base
salary, vesting immediately. An annual wellness stipend of $500 may be spent
on gym memberships, fitness equipment, or mental-health apps. Open enrollment
runs every November.

Section 6: Code of Conduct
--------------------------
Employees must treat colleagues, customers, and partners with respect.
Harassment or discrimination of any kind is grounds for disciplinary action
up to and including termination. Conflicts of interest must be disclosed to
HR. Confidential company information may not be shared outside the company
without written authorization.

Section 7: IT Security Standard
-------------------------------
All work devices must use full-disk encryption and the company-managed VPN
when accessing internal systems, including the employee records database.
Multi-factor authentication is mandatory for all corporate accounts.
Report suspected security incidents to security@acme.example.com within
24 hours.

Section 8: Performance Reviews
------------------------------
Performance reviews are held twice a year, in June and December. Each review
covers goal attainment, competencies, and career development. Merit-based
salary adjustments take effect in January following the December review
cycle.

Section 9: Expense Reimbursement
--------------------------------
Business expenses must be submitted in the expense system within 30 days of
being incurred, with itemized receipts for any expense over $25. Approved
expenses are reimbursed in the next payroll cycle. Daily meal allowance for
business travel is $75 domestic and $100 international.

Section 10: Contacting HR
-------------------------
The HR service desk is available Monday through Friday, 09:00-17:00, via the
HR portal or hr@acme.example.com. For payroll questions, contact
payroll@acme.example.com. Employee records corrections (address, banking,
emergency contacts) are made in the HR portal under "My Profile".
