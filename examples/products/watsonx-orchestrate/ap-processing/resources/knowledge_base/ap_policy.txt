NORTHWIND MANUFACTURING — ACCOUNTS PAYABLE POLICY
==================================================

Section 1: Purpose and Scope
-----------------------------
This policy governs the processing, validation, and payment of all vendor
invoices received by Northwind Manufacturing. It applies to every invoice
submitted against a Northwind purchase order, regardless of dollar amount.
The AP team is responsible for enforcing these rules consistently before any
payment is authorised or scheduled.

Section 2: Three-Way Matching Requirement
------------------------------------------
Every invoice must be matched against three independent documents before it
may be approved for payment: (1) the originating purchase order (PO),
(2) the goods-receipt or service-confirmation record in the ERP system,
and (3) the vendor invoice itself. A match is considered successful only when
the vendor ID, PO number, line-item quantities, and amounts agree across all
three documents within the tolerance band defined in Section 3. Invoices that
cannot be matched to a valid, open PO in the ERP are automatically routed for
human review and are never auto-approved.

Section 3: Amount-Mismatch Tolerance Band
------------------------------------------
Minor rounding and currency-conversion discrepancies are acceptable within the
following tolerance: the greater of ±2% of the PO line-item amount or ±$50,
whichever is larger. For example, a PO line for $1,000 permits an invoice
variance of up to $20 (2%); a PO line for $200 permits a variance of up to
$50 (the flat floor). Any mismatch that exceeds this band on any line item
routes the invoice to a human AP analyst for review and resolution. The
analyst must document the reason for the variance and obtain a written
approval from the relevant budget owner before payment proceeds.

Section 4: Duplicate-Invoice Rule
----------------------------------
An invoice is flagged as a potential duplicate if either of the following
conditions is true: (a) the same vendor ID and the same invoice number
appear on any previously received invoice, regardless of amount or date; or
(b) the same vendor ID, the same invoice amount, and the same invoice date
appear on any previously received invoice, regardless of invoice number.
Duplicate-flagged invoices are held and routed to a human AP analyst. The
analyst verifies with the vendor whether the submission is a reissuance,
a correction, or an inadvertent duplicate. Payment is blocked until the
analyst resolves the flag.

Section 5: Exception Routing
------------------------------
The following conditions always route an invoice to a human AP analyst and
are never subject to automatic approval or payment: (a) amount mismatches
outside the tolerance band defined in Section 3; (b) vendor ID or PO number
on the invoice does not match any active record in the ERP vendor master or
any open PO within the approved range; (c) a duplicate flag triggered under
Section 4; (d) a missing or unconfirmed goods-receipt record at the time of
matching; (e) any invoice from a vendor whose status in the ERP is marked
On-Hold or Inactive. Exceptions must be resolved within 5 business days to
avoid late-payment risk. The AP manager reviews all open exceptions weekly.

Section 6: Payment Scheduling
-------------------------------
Invoices that pass three-way matching within tolerance are scheduled for
payment according to the vendor's contracted payment terms (Net 15, Net 30,
or Net 45) as recorded in the ERP vendor master. Early payment is permitted
only when a vendor has an active early-payment discount agreement on file.
Payments are released in the Tuesday batch unless the due date falls within
2 business days, in which case an off-cycle payment may be requested.
