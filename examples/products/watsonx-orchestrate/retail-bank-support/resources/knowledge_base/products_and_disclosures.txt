RIVERSTONE BANK — PERSONAL DEPOSIT PRODUCTS AND DISCLOSURES HANDBOOK
======================================================================

Section 1: Riverstone Everyday Checking
----------------------------------------
The Riverstone Everyday Checking account is designed for day-to-day spending
and bill payment. It provides unlimited debit-card transactions, free access
to the Riverstone ATM network (over 2,000 locations nationwide), and mobile
check deposit through the Riverstone Banking app.

Monthly maintenance fee: $8.00 per statement cycle. The monthly maintenance
fee is waived when the account holder maintains a minimum daily balance of
$500 or receives at least one qualifying direct deposit of $250 or more per
statement cycle.

Overdraft handling: If a transaction exceeds the available balance, Riverstone
may, at its discretion, pay the item and assess an overdraft fee of $32.00 per
occurrence, or return the item and assess a returned-item fee of $32.00. No
more than three overdraft or returned-item fees will be charged per business
day. Customers may link a Riverstone savings account for overdraft protection
transfers at no per-transfer fee.

Minimum opening deposit: $25.00. No minimum ongoing balance requirement beyond
the fee-waiver thresholds described above.

Section 2: Riverstone High-Yield Savings
-----------------------------------------
The Riverstone High-Yield Savings account is an interest-bearing deposit
account intended for building personal savings. Interest is calculated on the
average daily balance and credited to the account on the last business day of
each statement cycle.

The Annual Percentage Yield (APY) is variable and is set at Riverstone Bank's
discretion; current rates are posted at riverstone.example.bank and are
subject to change without prior notice.

Monthly maintenance fee: $4.00 per statement cycle. The fee is waived when
the average daily balance remains at or above $300 for the entire statement
cycle.

Federal regulations limit certain types of transfers and withdrawals from
savings accounts. Riverstone High-Yield Savings accounts are subject to a
limit of six convenient transfers or withdrawals per statement cycle (including
online, telephone, and pre-authorized transfers). Excess-transaction fees of
$10.00 per transaction apply to each transfer or withdrawal above the limit
in a statement cycle.

Minimum opening deposit: $25.00.

Section 3: Riverstone Money Market
------------------------------------
The Riverstone Money Market account combines higher-yield interest with limited
check-writing privileges. Account holders receive a book of ten free checks per
year; additional check books are available for $5.00 per book.

Interest tiers (variable APY, subject to change):
  - Balance $0 – $9,999.99:    Tier 1 rate (posted at riverstone.example.bank)
  - Balance $10,000 – $49,999.99: Tier 2 rate
  - Balance $50,000 and above:  Tier 3 rate

Monthly maintenance fee: $12.00 per statement cycle. The fee is waived when
the average daily balance is $2,500 or more for the entire statement cycle.

The same six-transfer limit that applies to Riverstone High-Yield Savings
accounts applies to the Riverstone Money Market account. Excess-transaction
fees are $10.00 per transaction above the limit per statement cycle.

Minimum opening deposit: $1,000.00.

Section 5: Regulatory Disclosures
------------------------------------
5.1  Variable Rates.  Interest rates and Annual Percentage Yields for
Riverstone High-Yield Savings and Riverstone Money Market accounts are
variable and may be adjusted at any time at Riverstone Bank's sole discretion.
Past rates are not a guarantee of future rates. Current rates are available
at riverstone.example.bank or by calling Riverstone customer support.

5.2  Deposit Insurance.  Deposits held at Riverstone Bank are insured up to
the applicable limits established by the relevant deposit insurance authority
for your account type and ownership category. Riverstone Bank is a fictional
institution used for illustrative purposes; in a real deployment, you would
confirm your institution's actual insurer and coverage limits.
